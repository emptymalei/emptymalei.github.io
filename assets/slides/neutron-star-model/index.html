Metric of a spherical star

Line element of a static spherical object, \begin{equation} ds^2 = - e^{-2\Phi} dt^2 + e^{2\lambda} dr^2 + r^2 d\Omega^2 \end{equation} in which $d\Omega^2 := d\theta^2 + \sin^2\theta d\phi^2$.

1. spherical so not theta phi dependence;2. static so no time dependence;3. but can have scaling factor.

Energy momentum tensor

In the perfect fluid model, $T_{ab}$ \begin{equation}T_{ab} = (\rho + P)U_a U_b + P g_{ab}\end{equation}

Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation etc

\begin{align} \frac{d m(r)}{dr} & = 4\pi \rho(r) r^2 \\ \frac{d\Phi(r)}{dr} & = - \frac{m(r)+4\pi P(r) r^3}{r\left( r-2m(r) \right)} \\ \frac{dP(r)}{dr} & = -(\rho + P(r)) \frac{m(r) + 4\pi P(r) r^3}{r\left( r-2 m(r) \right)} \label{eq:OVEqn} \end{align}

Fourth eq n: EoS

a, Schematic slice through a neutron star. Letters N, n, p, e, μ refer to the presence of nuclei, fluid neutrons and protons, electrons and muons, respectively. At high enough densities the nuclei might deform and connect along certain directions to form extended tubes, sheets and bubbles of nuclear matter.
